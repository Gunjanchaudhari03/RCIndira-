What We Do



Proffessional Grooming

giving Rotaractors the opportunity to serve while building lasting bonds with fellow Rotaractors, the Professional Development Committee makes it all worthwhile by providing members with the opportunity to grow personally and professionally.

Women Empowerment

Women's empowerment and gender equality have continuously been a part of Rotaract. Here in Rotaract, we are treated equally, given responsibilities equally, and not looked down upon or underestimated. Most importantly, in the club, we, as women, feel much more secure because none of us is made to feel uncomfortable.

Outing and Games

Develops strategies for membership development and retention Monitors attendance Oversees the productions and distribution of the club bulletin Plans fellowship activities for the membership

Membership Development Director

A membership strategy or plan can help your club set reasonable goals for finding and keeping club members. You can find ideas in Strengthening Your Membership: Creating Your Membership Plan.

International Service

Many clubs associate with other clubs around the world for the initiatives taken by Rotaract, the ISD director has key role in building and maintaining relations. The International service director of Rotaract has got the job of handling all the international relations of the club.

Community Service

Primarily responsible for seeking opportunities to enhance knowledge and understanding of local community needs and problems Formulating and developing appropriate activities for serving the local community Responsible for initiating and planning one major activity each year which shall involve all or most of the club membership.

Public Relation Officer

This role is to work with a variety of audiences, including civic dignitaries, to inform the community about the Club, Rotary, its history, goals and accomplishments throughout the Rotary year.
